# Oscar Best Director Style Bank

Use this as a practical style bank for recent Best Director directions. Match the user's footage first; do not force a director reference onto unsuitable material.

## How To Use

1. Inspect subject, motion, light, texture, location, and emotional temperature.
2. Pick 2-3 plausible directions, then choose one primary direction.
3. Translate the direction into rhythm, palette, contrast, texture, sound, and camera treatment.
4. Verify newer winners before claiming the list is current.
5. Describe an adapted cinematic direction, not an exact imitation of a living artist.

## Direction Notes

- Christopher Nolan: large-scale clarity, structural tension, restrained palette, precise cuts, strong low-end sound, controlled contrast. Fits architecture, machines, crowds, night exteriors, or procedural suspense.
- Daniel Kwan and Daniel Scheinert: fast tonal shifts, playful montage, bold color contrast, emotional sincerity under chaos. Fits mixed media, family moments, absurd objects, kinetic handheld footage, or surreal edits.
- Jane Campion: tactile natural environments, quiet psychological pressure, muted earth tones, restrained pacing, attention to gesture and silence. Fits landscapes, interiors, faces, domestic tension, or slow observational footage.
- Chloe Zhao: natural light, wide landscapes, small human figures, documentary patience, warm-low contrast realism. Fits travel, nature, ordinary labor, roads, dusk, and reflective subjects.
- Bong Joon-ho: controlled blocking, tonal precision, social contrast, clean compositions, suspenseful pacing, sharp shifts from humor to dread. Fits interiors, stairs, class contrast, groups, or narrative irony.
- Alfonso Cuaron: long takes, immersive movement, naturalistic light, spatial continuity, atmospheric sound. Fits walking shots, vehicles, streets, water, crowds, or continuous movement.
- Guillermo del Toro: fairy-tale darkness, saturated production color, tactile texture, warm practical lights against cold shadows. Fits objects, night interiors, fantasy, rain, metal, glass, or ornate spaces.
- Damien Chazelle: musical rhythm, saturated stage light, energetic camera movement, romantic contrast, strong sound-image synchronization. Fits performance, city night, dance, traffic, neon, or montage.
- Alejandro G. Inarritu: harsh natural light, handheld intensity, survival texture, long-take pressure, weathered skin and environments. Fits wilderness, physical struggle, crowds, or emotionally heavy realism.
- Ang Lee: graceful restraint, emotional clarity, balanced compositions, delicate color separation, controlled movement. Fits intimate human scenes, landscape, memory, or quiet conflict.

## Selection Cautions

- Avoid high-energy montage styles when the footage is mostly static and fragile.
- Avoid glossy contrast when the user's reference asks for realism or documentary feeling.
- Preserve speech and natural sound when they carry story or atmosphere.
- Prefer a simpler grade over a famous style when the footage quality is low or compressed.
